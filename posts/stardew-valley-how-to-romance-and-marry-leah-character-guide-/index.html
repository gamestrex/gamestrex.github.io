How To Marry Leah In Stardew Valley?#

Just like in real life to marry Leah players will have to know her schedule, her like, her dislikes, and woo her. You will need to collect heart from Leah’s heart events and once you have enough hearts you will be able to marry Leah.

How To Complete All The Leah Hear Events In Stardew Valley?#

To get the first 2 hearts you will have to help Leah sell art online or suggest she have an art show in the town. Once you head into Leah’s house she will ask you a question and you will get 2 options, don’t select the “may I have a kiss” option. That option will make you seem like a creep and also not eligible for the 8 heart event. For the 4 heart event, you will have to choose the right response when you see Leah fighting with her ex over the phone. The right response will be choosing “NO, it had to be done” or “No, and your ex sounds like an idiot”. For the 6 heart event, Leah will give you a hand-made present at your farm. The 7 heart event is only available in Summer, Spring, or Autumn season. You will have to find Leah at Marnie’s farm and help her get some fruits The 8 heart event is directly linked to your response in the 2 heart event. If you suggested Leah hold an art show you will have to visit the art show between 3 pm to 5 pm. The art show won’t happen in Winter so you will have to wait till the spring season to attend it, but make sure you attend it in the given time frame. If you suggested she sell her art online just head to her home when she is starting to sell her art online. For the 10 hear event you will have to visit Mariane’s farm after 11 am and go for a picnic with her. Her ex will show up at the picnic, you can reason or argue with him or you can punch him, either way, you will be impressing Leah. The 14 hears event will have you painting your heart out on the canvas. Head out on a sunny day between 5 am – 8:20 am and you’ll have one event, in case you don’t get an event try on the next sunny day between 11:30 am – 2 pm. Just remember that a sunny day shouldn’t be a Sunday.
