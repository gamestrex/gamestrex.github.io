November 20, 2022 · 4 min · 805 words · John Tiedeman

The decision to ban PUBG Mobile and other 117 Chinese apps was taken in the interest of India’s sovereignty and integrity, defence and security, the government said in a statement. “This move will safeguard the interests of crores of Indian mobile and internet users. This decision is a targeted move to ensure safety, security and sovereignty of Indian cyberspace,” said a statement from the ministry. This is not the first time Indian banned Chinese apps. The Government of India recently blocked 59 apps including the popular video-sharing app, TikTok. Later, the government banned 47 more apps. “The compilation of these data, its mining and profiling by elements hostile to national security and defence of India, which ultimately impinges upon the sovereignty and integrity of India, is a matter of very deep and immediate concern which requires emergency measures,” the statement said. Besides PUBG Mobile, the most popular Chinese apps that have been banned by the Indian Government are Baidu, APUS Launcher Pro. The government claimed that all these (now banned) apps were involved in some such activities, which could pose a threat to the sovereignty and integrity of the country, security of the country, security of the state and public order, etc.
