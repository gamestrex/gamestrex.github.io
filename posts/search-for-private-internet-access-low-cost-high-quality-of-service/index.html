A range of products and services are available.#

Private Internet access with an active internet connection. Private Internet access. When you open the PIA, it looks the same as we’ve seen since the company had revamped its app in January 2019. You get a simple panel under the right corner of the screen. This case is fixed to this location and can’t be moved. That’s not a choice that I particularly like, because I feel too weak, but that one isn’t terribly bad given the proper structure of the room. There’s a large button on the left and right, with an indicator of the location that takes you to a different screen. A different type of screen, such as the various apex-language or country enshrined and annotated, allows you to select from one iA/apex/apex/i/afus/apex/i//wo and so on. In a given country with multiple locations, the downward-facing arrow appears to the left of the country name. You can make the list more compact by opening multiple options. Every country has its ping time over 100 milliseconds. A heart icon is used to make them certain locations preferred. Overall, this is a nice design. PIA organizes the list in two different ways: latency or name. That is a lot easier for people with specific interest in speed rather than those with specific interests in a specific country. Last time around, the app was able to improve results by latency, which meant some quick navigation when looking for a specific country. After you’ve connected, the apps’ last panel shows you your actual IP address and the VPN address you’re using. This is a very simple view. If you click the lower-facing arrow at the bottom, you see a ton of extra modules such as a fast-connected area with countries like France, Germany, USA, and the United Kingdom. There are also tiles to use bandwidth, performance graphs, data encryption and handshake settings being used, and basic settings control. Click the bookmark icon on any of these and they join the compact view. You can add or remove any of these options according to your taste. As we last looked, PIA has added several additional features, including split tunneling, multi-hop options, and dedicated IP addresses. You can use an automated mechanism to set up a standard and automatically connect to the VPN if it is a open Wi-Fi server. It would be nice to see this automation feature expand to look even more robust, like adding the option to choose a specific location. Having said all of which, PIA has significantly increased its country count since last we looked, from 33 to 78; however, 35 of those are virtual servers where the physical hardware isn’t in the country it claims to be. These spots usually are similar to Saudi Arabia and China, where preparing the servers is a problem. You can read more about these geo-locations on the PIA site. As at PIA, there is a remote control, an ad tracker and malware blocker that defaults. You can choose between OpenVPN and WireGuard connections and change your options to the encryption of data and the handshake of OpenVPN. OpenVPN has an average byte of memory, but if you want, you could choose AES-256 (gmc or CBC). WireGuard has no option, as all the encryption and authentication options are well standardized under one setup.

Performance#

PIA has great speed. After testing, the service maintained 41 percent of its base speed on five locations. In Britain and Germany, speed was exceptionally rapid, keeping 66 per cent and 58 per cent a year. IDG PIA has good speed. In the test using OpenVPN standard, the service maintained 36.95 percent of the base speed across five locations and several test speeds. That’s a slip from the 41 percent to the first time we saw last time. However, PIA is still fast enough to fall in the top 10 of our speed tests. Speeds in UK and Germany were particularly fast, and Australia was reliable, all in all, hitting around 15 Mbps. All speeds were two-digits, so all can be used as a dragstream or other common occurrence.

Today its focus is not on advertising, c’est-à-dire a user-based security software such as VPNs and other specialized software. Kapes boss is Ido Erlichman and his name is Teddy Sagi. Kape has recently started a VPN buying spree. It owns in part CyberGhost, ZenMate and Intego, too. Conclusion#

PIA is a genuinely good VPN service, which means it’s the lowest price of any VPN. If you don’t need more information, there’s no better way of getting better VPNs. But the combination of good speed, an app and a good price make this service a good service. *Private Internet Access uses virtual location servers where one server is located in a country, but currently lies in another country.
