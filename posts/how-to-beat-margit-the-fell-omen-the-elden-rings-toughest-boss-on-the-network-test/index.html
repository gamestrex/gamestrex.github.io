Magic blade – In the first half of the fight, Margit primarily attacks with two weapons: the staff in his right hand and a glowing magic blade (which still does physical damage) summoned in her left arm. The staff attack is a relatively slow, heavy hit, but Margit summons the sword to deliver a fast, close-range combo even if he’s in the recovery animation. This is the assault that gave me the most trouble, because he used it always when I thought that I had an opening. Be safe; try not to enter one attack before sweeping away your shield or putting up your shield. Thrower – When you retreat to heal, Margit loves to summon two of those magic blades again and throw them at you. If you’re not able to drink from your sloppy face and drink, strafe towards you so he can’t hit you. If you are ready for them, they’re easy to dodge. Combos – The danger of the fight is Margit’s unpredictable combo patterns. Sometimes he’ll swing once or twice, and sometimes he’ll bring a spinning attack to a multi-hit sequence. Do not attack her. Just block or dodge backwards, if you don’t think you are out of range, the staff can’t help you. I think that blocking is easier, because he’ll swing multiple times in a row. Generally, you want to keep your distance away and wait for his hammer jump, which gives you a much more simple opening. Hammer jump – This is Margit’s heaviest hit, but this is the best window when you dodge properly. After his health got worse, Margit will summon a giant glowing hammer with his left hand. After a very big explosion, he’ll swing it at you, but when you reach the end of the stage, he’ll leap out and turn it back on the other side. You want to bait this out, then roll towards him at the last minute, dodging through the impact and reaching close to Margit. This will give you a few seconds of security to wail him.

Can you take a spirit sword to distract Margit?#

Spirit Ashes are Elden Ring’s version of summonable NPCs like Solaire, but this time they are items you can buy and reuse throughout the game. You can buy two ashes from Merchant Kale near Limgrave: the Lone Wolf and the Noble Sorcerer Ashes. The Lone Wolf Ashes are very good for this fight because they spawn three wolves that allow Margit to move for a long time. You aren’t counting on them for damages. They only need to live alive long enough to escape your aggro, so you can snag some hits.
