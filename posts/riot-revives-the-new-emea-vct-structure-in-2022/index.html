Riot Games continue to prove they’re one of the best in the business when it comes to organizing their esports events and hyping their audience. Despite global famines that have eroded and crashed the world of sports, this company hasn’t only launched one of today’s most popular shooters but also completed a long, year-long, Olympic-Muslim Championship (VW-Tour). And now, just days after the title of the Champion, they are back with incredible new information. The new unveiled information is concerned about the upcoming VCT 2022 season, where Riot Games revealed their new VCT regional format for the EMEA region.

World’s biggest FPS, Valorant is the world’s biggest Esports.#

The Valorant team was a massive news bomb and there was so much to recover. A quick dive was needed. In the past two weeks, the European region has emerged as a leading country in Valorant Champions. Acend, one of the top Valorant teams from Europe, won the trophy and became the first world champion in Valorant. Photo Credit | Riot Games. The exceptional performance and the interesting dynamic of the EMEA teams has been rewarded for a new level of interest. Riot Games have been following this development very closely. That was one of the reasons they decided to change the EMEA ecosystem in 2022 so they could add more layers. This should allow more players, from amateurs to pro players, to participate in the competition Valorant scene and to play it big. The Valorant staff said that in one of their recent announcements: “There were two main things that went into effect.” In 2022, we aspire to become the world’s largest aficionados and its impressive performance proves its potential. Eventually, EMEA is evolving to be a powerhouse region. Though they are just kicking things off with the EMEA region, for obvious reasons, these same changes will likely impact all over the Valorant regions over time. This proved to be a complete success, even though Valorant Champions Tour was a bit of an experiment at first. It’s great seeing Riot expand on an already tested formula and I’ve been excited to see how it’s going to be 2022.

A construction of the VCT OEMEA 2022 structure.#

If you remember this years VCT pyramid structure, you know that there were only three layers of VCT Challengers, Masters, and finally Champions. In next season, Valorant will receive two additional layers, Valorant regional and Game Changers, but also VRC amateur circuits. Photo Credits | Riot Games

The VRL (Valorants Regional Leagues) has no sexy agenda.#

The EMEA region will be starting with several UMTS. Each of the regional leagues will be covered by one of the Riots’ partner organisers. This is the actual list of EMEA XLRs that are coming next.

A LVP controlled Spain (Players from Portugal and Italy, as well as others to compete in this league) and operated by Spain. France (Players within Benelux can compete in this league as well) operated by Freaks 4U Gaming. UK, Ireland and the Nordics operated by Promod Esports. Poland + EE (Players Israel, too) operated by FRENZY. A government-owned company, GIG Me, handles a CIS. MENA operated by Calyx Gaming. In order to maintain its own security, the security of competition is an important part of the Turkish economy. Freaks 4U Gaming operates DACH.

Those who are on the EMEA game have their fingers smashed.#

We’ll know that the Game Changers series will continue in 2022. The VCT EMEA regions Game Changers will have three tournaments to attend in February, May and September. The team which succeeds here will qualify for the Global Game Changers in November. The Game Changers Academy is among the new innovations. It’s planned to be a safe space for new teams to practice and compete throughout the year.

The Amateur Circuits (VC) (VDC) will be the first to get started.#

In order to help promote and develop Valorant talent for the masses, Riot plans to create a new series of amateur competitions called VRCs. This should boost the amateur ecosystem by new heights and make it possible to the young and talenty Valorant players to look after themselves and play in competitive situations. It’s going to be an VRC season where teams accumulate circuit points, and those points eventually determine the final winners.
