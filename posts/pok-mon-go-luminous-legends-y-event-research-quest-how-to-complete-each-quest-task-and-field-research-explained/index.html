Thank you to SilphScience from reddit for the help with this information. The centrepiece of Luminous Legends Y’s second half is the release of Sylveon - the fairy-type Eeveelution - into Pokémon Go. Just like the previous Eeveelutions, Sylveon has both a name trick and a unique evolution method, which you can learn about on our Eevee evolution page. To celebrate Sylveon’s release, Vaporeon, Jolteon, Flareon, Espeon and Umbreon will all be appearing in three-star raids. While Eevee will be available in one-star raids and Yveltal will continue to appear in five-star raids. You’ll also be able to fight the Mega Evolutions of Altaria, Ampharos and Houndoom in Mega Raids. The 7km egg pool has been altered once more to include the following Pokémon all on Tier One:

Eevee Cleffa Igglybuff Togepi Azurill Spritzee Swirlix

Fairy-type Pokémon have also started reappearing more frequently in the wild again, so keep an eye out for the following:

Clefairy Jigglypuff Eevee Togetic Marill Snubbull Ralts Spritzee Swirlix

There will still be a number of dark-type Pokémon lurking about as well. We collectively have until 11:59pm UTC on Sunday, 23rd May to defeat 25 million Team Go Rocket Grunts. You can keep check in the worldwide progress for this challenge by visiting the official Pokémon Go Twitter for the occasional update. If we manage to defeat all of these villainous trainers, then a triple Catch XP bonus, the release of shiny Galarian Zigzagoon and for Galarian Zigzagoon to appear in one-star raids untill the event ends. The Season of Mythical Wishes continues with the Winter Holiday event, which has brought Mega Glalie to Pokémon Go. Don’t forget to partake in the new Go Battle League season. Elsewhere, be sure to use Daily Adventure Incense for the chance of encountering Galarian Articuno, Galarian Zapdos and Galarian Moltres.
An important fact to remember about the Luminous Legends Y event is that, while it is running for those two weeks, it’s also divided into two halves - each with their own set of releases, raid bosses and other bonuses. The first half, running from Tuesday 18th May at 10am (local time) till Monday, 24th May at 8pm (local time), is focused upon dark-type Pokémon and Team Go Rocket. Meanwhile, the second half, is themed around the release of Sylveon, the fairy-type eevolution, and is running from Tuesday, 25th May at 10am (local time) to Monday, 31st May at 8pm (local time). As mentioned, the Luminous Legends Y timed research throughout both halves of the events, meaning you definitely have enough to complete each of the seven steps and every individual challenge. Just remember - this research quest will vanish when the event ends, so, if you want all of the rewards, make sure you complete it before the deadline. Hope you enjoy the Luminous Legends Y event!
