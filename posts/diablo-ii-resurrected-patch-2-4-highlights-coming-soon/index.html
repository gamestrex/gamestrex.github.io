CLASS BALANCE CHANGES CHANGES.#

After so many requests from the community, we got excited to share new balance changes with your favorites in Diablo II: Resurrected! As fans we were keen to find new ways to play, we had no less experience. This is so special as the last patch, which had been released on March 23 2010 to make sure the classes were always in balance. After eleven years, were making new changes to broaden character expression and retain knowledge and expertise. These goals are key. We’re looking for underused skills, estimating casting delays, making quality-of-life improvements, and more to achieve these goals. We want to bring you more creative with the builds you want to play, but we’re not interested in reinventing the wheel and deciding their core goals. Before a dive into the specifics, I can try to jump into some of the philosophies behind class change.

AMAZON#

To increase bow game, we’re considering playing the same game in the bow and crossbow tree.

ASSASSIN#

The Assassin reveals that evil was borne with ease. However, in order to learn these lessons, the Martial Arts will learn new things. Martial Arts skills become increasingly difficult for the performer in higher difficulty, so we are thinking about the process of getting a Combo Point. Also, there aren’t often used many Trap skills. So, were re-evaluating synergy requirements to promote more trap build and damage scaling buffs.

BARBARIAN#

The barbarians are unmatched. To this day, we see the potential to add more diversity to Combat Skills. We’re looking into adding more possibilities with Combat Skills such as Leap Attack or Grim Ward, but also looking at new skills to promote more reasons to spend points on certain Combat Skills. Also, we think Throwing Barbarians are more viable, so we’re looking at enhancing both the Double Throw and Throwing Mastery’s skills.

DRUID#

It is clear, the bestial ferocity of the Druid makes everyone tremble. To do that, we find exciting opportunities to level up some aspects of this class. From our point of view, Druid Fire is underperforming. We re-evaluate Casting Delays for Fire Skills, and explore improvements to certain of their Physical Damage components and improve synergies. We are looking at potential changes affecting any of the above skills that are underused. We also want to improve the controls for Arctic Blast, like to allow more free form while channeling and no longer locking your target attacks onto one enemy at a time. Speaking of Summon skills, we believe Summons in high difficulty are weak in high difficulty. On the front, was evaluating health gains per level, health-in-life improvement, damage-based benefits and new synergies for the Ravens, Wolves and Vines. Finally, we’re evaluating the Werebear skills, focusing mostly on the synergy improvements.

NECROMANCER#

The wrath of the holy zealot is glorious. Despite this, we believe certain Combat Skills are underused. Were studying ways to utilise lesser-used skills (also Fist of the Heavens) more easily used, therefore players have more time to spend the skills to unlock these skills. We see a chance to scale the skills of Aura-Offfensive’s. This includes upgrading the AoE damage-urans to make them more effective in a close range. We also want to improve Thorns, so that they can scale and deal more reliable damage at higher levels.

SORCERESS#

The elemental magic masters are very powerful as these mages. We’re very satisfied with the Sorceress current status, but there are some opportunities to improve. In the fight against wars, we hope that the weapons are arguably more diverse than the other skills: Frozen, Shiver, and Chilling, so our advantages have increased. We can make up an extra amount of build possible to use lesser used Lightning skills by learning more skill synergies. Lastly, did also review Fire skills, to improve underused methods like Hydra. As with Arctic Blast, we’re going to try to improve the ability of player control to improve Inferno to make it easier to use. Resign and go with us!
