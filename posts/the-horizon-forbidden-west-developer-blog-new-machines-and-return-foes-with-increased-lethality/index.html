In the early years of Horizon Zero Dawn, Principal Machine Designer Blake Politeski worked with Guerrilla. Together with Asset Art Chief Maxim Fleury, their teams (and others) have been working on some great additions for the coming sequel. We were going to explore a new area in the world of Horizon, begins Blake. It was interesting to find out which types of machines would live in different ecological zones and how they would interact with each other, their environment, and of course humans. Machines are so-called caretakers of the world, so it’s always interesting to see how they perform their roles and what the player can do to protect them from these behaviors. In the initial stages of Horizon Zero Dawns, we had some wonderful ideas, of course not everything would play. But now with Horizon Forbidden West we could share one-two, exciting ideas that previously weren’t feasible in new and interesting concepts! Maxim elaborates: Blakes team provides a design document for our general looks, explains what type of equipment the machine has, and what kind of damage it can do. I have two sets of drawings and one sets of drawings, yet, the real visual design is moved from the Robotics team to the Weapons Project team. Concept Artists then create a 3-D concept which goes through many reviews, through which various art direction, game design, animation, and technical art teams will be created, till all are happy. Look, rig, and animate a model to see if everything works as needed, and the modelers on the robotic team will work on it, adding all the details in. After several iterations with different teams, the model is animated, painted, textured, and reviewed again before becoming added to the game, and replaces the temporary model. The process for the development of each machine takes a long time. Expanding the Machine Catalog. With over 25 different machines in Horizon Zero Dawn, finishing them was never easy. I started small, and began to define different roles in the first game, says Blake. The Watcher is like a security camera; the Ravager is an enforcer, and so forth. Once those base roles were established, we were able to build on that by mixing in robot designs, unique capabilities and features, and of course cool weapons and attacks. We’re expanding this catalog further with Horizon Forbidden West. For example, we knew we wanted a slightly bigger flying machine than the Glinthawk, which resulted in the Sunwing but it needed a place and a role in the world. The idea to see the planet when its floats and primitive birds were formed was created using the thought of collecting solar energy during a sunny season. This then created an interesting gameplay dynamic: Sunwings are vulnerable when they harvest solar energy, but also more alert to potential predators when they are idle. There are certain things that come into play, as well as the tools we have to define. We always want to ensure that the machines are connected, and that they serve a different purpose. Therefore, the whole machines (eco-system) is identical to the real world. Guerrilla always wanted to create a genuine world. This includes constructing real machines in the surrounding world. I don’t think that the design language from Horizon Zero Dawn has changed greatly for us, explains Maxim. As well as changing our machines, we are trying to take advantage of different equipment that make the world awe-inquire for our players. The machines in Horizon Forbidden West feature much more functionality this time around, is a challenge from a design perspective, but has a chance of making a unique experience for the player during a different encounter. New and dangerous Foes. In an earlier blog we discussed some of Aloys’s abilities and combat moves that she learns in the Forbidden West. Some will probably come in handy against stronger, faster and deadlier machines. Aloy has many different weapons and ammunition, and she will need all of them to beat the different machines! Without spoiling something, every machine has many ways to win. We tried the machine design to make players feel clear, and we added textures to show the weak points or the interactive components. You must closely study each machine so that you can find different ways to approach it. Blake adds: A lot of machines have various weapons and behaviors that can be used for a benefit. This isn’t just in shooting off parts and using them, it’s also possible that some machines have more passive states, where the player can sway their opponent. Because of this, Aloy will have to keep up with it, so there are new movement mechanics that help her out. In the machine battle, actions like climbing or using a Smoke Bomb can give Aloy an edge, but more importantly, players can use a system that improved both well and well. Apart from using its tusks to close attacks, he has also different elemental weapons. In the 1960s, a huge siege tower was used for the attack of fortresses and war elephants; hard to destroy, slow, and high firepower is still scarce. It inspired the idea for the Tremortusk, a well-armed, but slow machine, with an unstoppable force.
